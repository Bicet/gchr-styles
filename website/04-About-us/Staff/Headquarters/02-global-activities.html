Global Activities Department

We coordinate the implementation of joint activities that are the result of the collaboration of the GC seven regional programmes, including the Global Classroom, the International Conference on Human Rights and Democratisation, the Global Internship Programme, the Global Campus Human Rights Journal and publication of the best-awarded theses. We also support capacity building projects such as the EU-project for the creation of the Human Rights Centre at the National University of Timor-Leste in cooperation with GC Asia-Pacific.

Global Campus Project Manager (Head of Department)

Valentina Abita

Valentina joined the Global Campus in November 2013. As Project Manager she coordinates the Global Activities (joint conferences, research projects, internships, visibility/advocacy initiatives, etc.) in cooperation with the colleagues of the seven GC regional programmes.

She gained 10-year professional experience in project management and networking with EU institutions. In her previous roles, Valentina worked as Project Manager in the field of civil society participation and social inclusion and as Assistant in the external relations department of the Permanent Representation of Ireland to the EU during the EU Irish Presidency in 2013.

She has also collaborated with different international NGOs, institutions and other stakeholders on EU projects and policy and advocacy initiatives.

Valentina holds a Master’s degree in International relations from the University of Bologna and a Post-graduate Diploma in European studies from the European College of Parma.

Project Officer

Project Manager - Development of HR Education and Research at UNTL

Adriano Remiddi

Adriano is an experienced practitioner in the field of international cooperation for higher education. He is committed to promoting democracy and HR education in post-conflict, developing and transitional societies.

In the course of ten years spent in Southeast Europe and later Asia-Pacific, he has developed a distinctive mix of academic and managerial skills and gained a diversified and progressively responsible professional experience.

Since 2019 Adriano has led an EU-funded capacity building project aimed at establishing the Human Rights Centre of the National University of Timor-Leste (UNTL).

Previously he worked as Academic Tutor and subsequently Coordinator of the GC South East Europe/ERMA on behalf of the Institute for Central-Eastern and Balkan Europe/University of Bologna (IECOB).

As IECOB research fellow in regional studies he also lectured internationally, published, organised scientific conferences and participated in panels as speaker. Since 2014 he has been president of the alumni association of the MA in Eastern European Studies of the University of Bologna.
